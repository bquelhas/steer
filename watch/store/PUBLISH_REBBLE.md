Notes:
- Maneuver screenshots ship for `aplite_*` (144×168, b/w), `basalt_*` (144×168),
  `chalk_*` (180×180, round), `diorite_*` (144×168, b/w) and `emery_*` (200×228).
  Each set is left / right / roundabout / straight / arrive. The background red is
  recoloured to the mockup's `#ff313e` (basalt/chalk/emery; aplite/diorite are b/w).
- **emery capture note:** the emery emulator only boots under SDK **4.9.77** (the
  4.9.169/4.17 emery `qemu_micro_flash.bin` is an ELF, not a cooked raw flash image, so
  QEMU can't boot it). But 4.9.77 lacks `light_set_color()` (an RGB-backlight API added in
  4.9.169), so a throwaway build with the three `light_set_color()` calls neutralised was
  used only to boot the emulator and capture frames (`STEER_SCREENSHOT_4977` guard, since
  reverted). The **shipped .pbw is the normal 4.9.169 build** — the guard never reaches it.
  The real-watch CloudPebble relay path (`scripts/emery_real_relay.sh`) is kept for future
  use but was not needed. Old pre-recolour drafts are parked in
  `docs/screenshots/deferred_11_emery/`.
- aplite/basalt/chalk/diorite/emery were generated by injecting `NAV_TURN` + `NAV_TEXT` + `NAV_ETA`
  AppMessages into the emulator with `scripts/inject_nav.py` (the `pebble` CLI can only
  launch the app — it can't push AppMessages). To regenerate: install the app on the
  emulator, then `inject_nav.py <platform> <0-4>` and screenshot.
- Icons/banner are editable drafts — banner source `store/banner.svg` (wordmark in
  the watch's LECO font), icon source `docs/icon_car_red.svg`. Swap for other art.

## 3. Listing text

**Type:** Watchapp
**Title:** `Steer`
**Category:** `Tools & Utilities`
**Website:** `https://github.com/bquelhas/pebble-steer`
**Source code:** `https://github.com/bquelhas/pebble-steer`
**Support email:** optional (defaults to your Rebble account email)

**Description** (≤1600 chars):

```
STEER mirrors your phone's turn-by-turn navigation onto your Pebble: the next maneuver icon, distance, street name and ETA - glanceable on your wrist, so your phone can stay in your pocket.
What I wanted was a nav app that fits better with pebble design philosophy

Install the free Steer companion app from github (website link) on your Android phone; it reads navigation from your map app and forwards each maneuver to the watch.

Features:
- Next-maneuver arrow with distance and street
- ETA display
- Per-turn vibration, so you feel each turn without looking
- Custom background colour with automatic text contrast
- Automatic night backlight (red-tinted on Pebble Time 2) to protect night vision
- Launch a saved favourite destination straight from the watch

Reads navigation from Google Maps, OsmAnd, CoMaps and Organic Maps.

Works on Pebble Time / Time Steel, Pebble Time Round, Pebble 2 and Pebble Time 2.

Please let me know of any bugs

Open source - mostly vibecoded
```

**Release notes (1.1.0):**

```
- Pebble Classic / Pebble 2 (aplite) now supported.
- Smoother card-style page transitions and an odometer-style distance countdown.
- Night red backlight stays on the whole time the app is open.
```

## 4. Submission steps

1. Sign in at https://dev-portal.rebble.io.
2. New submission → **Watchapp**. Enter title, website and source URLs.
3. Pick category **Tools & Utilities**.
4. Upload **large icon**, **small icon** and **banner**.
5. Upload **screenshots** per platform from `docs/screenshots/` (aplite, basalt,
   chalk, diorite, emery — 5 each), then paste the **description** and **release notes**.
6. Upload the **.pbw** (`watch/build/watch.pbw`).
7. Submit. If prompted, download the bundle and email it to support@rebble.io.
   Review is normally quick; you get an email when it's live.
